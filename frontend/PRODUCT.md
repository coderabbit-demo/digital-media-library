# Product

## Register

product

## Users

People who read and listen across media — books, music, audiobooks, podcasts — and
want one place to track what they're into and see what friends are into. Used in
short, frequent sessions (check the feed, shelve something, jot a quick note/reply,
rate a finish), on desktop and phone, often in spare moments (commute, couch, before
bed). The job: keep a personal library organized by shelf and follow a lightweight
social feed of what people are currently on.

## Product Purpose

A multi-media "currently reading/listening" tracker with a Goodreads-style activity
feed: discover trending items and search across providers, shelve them in a private
library (Want to Read · Currently Reading · Read · Did Not Finish), post updates with
notes, reply in threads, rate, and like. Success = it's fast and pleasant to keep
your library current and to follow the feed, with cover art and people front-and-center.

## Brand Personality

Media-forward, warm, and confident. Three words: **vivid, tactile, unfussy.** The art
(covers, album/poster artwork, avatars) is the hero; chrome stays quiet so content
glows. Warmth comes from a single amber accent and type — not from busy surfaces.
Feels like a late-night listening room: matte dark, warm dial-glow highlights.

## Anti-references

- Material Design's component look (filled tonal buttons, rip—the system we're leaving).
- Cream/parchment "cozy bookish" skins; warm-tinted near-white surfaces.
- Generic SaaS dashboard (navy + gray cards, hero-metric template, identical card grids).
- Spotify-green / Letterboxd-blue reflexes — we use amber, deliberately.

## Design Principles

1. **Art leads, chrome recedes.** Covers/artwork and people are the brightest things on
   screen; surfaces and controls are quiet so content carries the color.
2. **One warm accent, used sparingly.** Amber marks the single most important action or
   state in a view; everywhere else is neutral.
3. **Calm density.** Show plenty without clutter — tight type, real spacing rhythm, few
   borders, no nested cards.
4. **Fast and legible first.** AA contrast everywhere; the feed and library load and
   scan quickly; motion is purposeful and respects reduced-motion.
5. **Same system, two skins.** Light and dark are one token set; dark is the signature.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA: body text ≥ 4.5:1, large/UI text ≥ 3:1, visible focus rings, full keyboard
paths. Every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative. Don't rely on color
alone (e.g. likes/ratings pair color with icon/label). Light + dark via system preference.
